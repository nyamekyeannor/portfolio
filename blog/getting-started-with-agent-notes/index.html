Start With The Mess

A useful technical post usually starts with something concrete: an error, a rough demo, a decision, or a tradeoff. Keep that detail. It gives the reader a reason to trust the lesson.

Turn Notes Into Shape

Use a simple structure: what you tried, what broke, what changed, and what someone else should do differently. That is enough for a strong first draft.

Make It Easy To Maintain

When you publish the post, add the entry to `blogPosts` in `content/site-data.js`. If the URL changes, update it there first.
